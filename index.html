Research

Age-Related Changes in Emotion Processing

My research focuses on how emotion recognition and visual representation develop across the lifespan. I investigate the developmental trajectories of emotional understanding, examining when and how children acquire the ability to recognize and interpret emotions in others, and how their mental representations of emotional expression evolve over time.

Read more about my research →

  • Key method: Drawing-based approach to track visual concepts of emotional expressions
  • Emotions studied: Happiness, sadness, anger, fear, disgust, pride, jealousy, shame, guilt, and embarrassment
